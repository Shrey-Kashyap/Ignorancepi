SPARSH CASE STUDY: THE APPRENTICE PROJECT
Holistic Development
Holistic education is an approach to education that focuses on the development of the whole
person, including their intellectual, emotional, social, physical, and spiritual well-being. It aims
to foster the growth of students in all aspects of their being, rather than solely focusing on
academic achievement.
Holistic education also recognizes the importance of experiential and hands-on learning. It
encourages students to actively participate in their own learning process, engage in critical
thinking, and apply their knowledge to real-world situations. It often includes activities such as
group discussions, projects, field trips, and practical experiences.
Skills acquired through holistic development are often referred to as 21st-century skills.
Importance of holistic education
Interconnectedness: Holistic education encourages students to make connections across
different disciplines and develop a system-thinking approach to problem-solving, enabling them
to address complex issues such as climate change, global inequality, etc.
Well-being and Resilience: The 21st century is marked by increased stress, mental health issues,
and a fast-paced lifestyle. Holistic education emphasizes the well-being of students, addressing
their physical, emotional, and spiritual needs.
Collaboration and Communication: It emphasizes teamwork, cooperation, and effective
communication, preparing students to work harmoniously with diverse groups and navigate
cultural differences.
Lifelong Learning and Adaptability: It nurtures a love for learning, curiosity, and a growth
mindset, enabling individuals to embrace change, learn new skills, and thrive in a rapidly
evolving world.
Underprivileged students missing out on holistic education
Students coming from underprivileged(economical/social) background rely on government
schools or private schools which do not charge high fees. These schools lack basic infrastructure
which makes learning difficult. Already lacking in academic capabilities, enough importance
isnt given to holistic development. Also, the economic condition of the family and the social
environment of the communities the students come from makes it even more difficult to acquire
any 21st-century skills.
Unemployability: Aspiring Minds National Employability Report indicates that ~47% of Indian
graduates are unemployable purely due to a lack of 21st-century skills.
Digital Illiteracy: According to the National Statistical Organization(NSO), only 20% of Indians
above the age of 5 years have basic digital literacy.
Financial Illiteracy: Based on a survey by the National Centre for Financial Education in 2020,
only 27% of Indians are financially literate.
About The Apprentice Project (TAP)
The Apprentice Project (TAP) is a for-impact organization determined to transform
Government/low-income schools by offering holistic development for all children. It uses
choice-based learning (Visual arts, Performing arts, or STEM) to develop 21st-century skills.
How TAP is helping to build 21st-century skills
The Apprentice Project (TAP) has built an AI and NLP-powered WhatsApp chatbot that uses co-
curricular electives and self-learning as a medium to develop 21st-century skills for middle and
high school students from low-income families in India. The chatbot provides video-based
electives (Coding, Science Lab, Visual and Performance Arts, Financial and Digital Literacy) and
prompts students to perform activities on their own at their own pace via personalized (AI) bot-
based nudges and content.
They have developed co-curricular self-learning courses on coding, science, visual and
performance arts, and digital and financial literacy, disseminated to students via their
WhatsApp chatbot - TAP Buddy. These courses aligned to 21st-century skills consist of multiple
video-based (each under 10 minutes) activities created by TAP for students to self-learn. At the
end of each activity, the student submits a video or photo via the bot to showcase their
learnings from the week's activity.
